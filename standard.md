# Open Mastery Standard
**Version 2.0 — March 2026**
*Leslie Warren / Kraft Kollab*

---

## Purpose

The Open Mastery Standard defines what it means to genuinely understand something. It establishes the conceptual criteria for recognizing mastery and the epistemic principles that govern valid claims of understanding.

The Standard is not an assessment instrument. It does not prescribe specific tasks, scoring methods, or classroom procedures. It defines the conditions that any evidence of mastery must satisfy, regardless of how that evidence is gathered or evaluated.

The Standard functions as a stable conceptual anchor. Assessment frameworks, grading systems, and instructional designs may evolve. The definition of mastery should not change with each implementation.

---

## Definition of Mastery

> **Mastery is the sustained coherence of understanding derived from mechanism, demonstrated through application to novel contexts, and confirmed by stability across variation and time.**

Four conditions must all be met:

**Mechanism.** The learner understands the underlying principle, rule, or causal structure that governs the domain — not merely what to do, but why it works.

**Application to novel contexts.** The learner can use the mechanism to produce correct results in situations not previously encountered during instruction.

**Stability.** The learner's understanding persists across time. A single demonstration cannot establish mastery. Understanding that is present on one occasion but absent weeks later was never stable enough to constitute mastery — it was an activation, not an acquisition.

**Coherence.** The learner's understanding holds together across variation. When the same mechanism appears in different contexts or in combination with other concepts, the learner's reasoning remains consistent and correct.

All four conditions must be observable for a mastery claim to be valid. Partial evidence indicates progress within the learning process, not mastery.

---

## What Mastery Is Not

**Mastery is not recall.** Reproducing stored information when prompted does not indicate understanding of the governing mechanism.

**Mastery is not procedural skill.** Executing learned steps correctly on familiar problem types is not mastery unless the learner can also explain why the procedure works and apply it in contexts requiring adaptation.

**Mastery is not compliance.** Completing assignments, attending class, and submitting work on time are logistical behaviors. They create opportunities for learning but are not demonstrations of understanding.

**Mastery is not a score.** No numerical score, percentage, or letter grade constitutes proof of mastery. Scores are compressed summaries of performance on particular instruments at particular times.

---

## Properties of Genuine Understanding

**Explanatory Capacity.** A learner who genuinely understands can explain the governing mechanism in their own language — a constructed account, not a retrieved one.

**Transferability.** Genuine understanding travels. It functions in contexts beyond the specific examples used during instruction.

**Durability.** Genuine understanding persists. It does not evaporate after the assessment, the unit, or the semester.

**Discriminative Precision.** Genuine understanding includes knowing the boundaries of a mechanism: when it applies, when it does not, and why.

**Integrative Structure.** A learner who understands individual concepts but cannot articulate how they connect has an inventory of understandings, not a coherent knowledge structure.

---

## Principles of Mastery Evidence

Mastery is a claim about a cognitive state. Cognitive states are not directly observable — they must be inferred from behavior. The following principles govern how this inference should be made.

**Evidence must be observable.** Claims of mastery must be supported by evidence that can be observed, documented, and evaluated by someone other than the learner. Self-report is not sufficient.

**Evidence must reveal mechanism.** Observable evidence must reveal the learner's understanding of the governing mechanism, not merely their ability to produce correct answers.

**Evidence must span time.** A single observation cannot establish that understanding is stable. Evidence must come from multiple occasions separated by meaningful intervals.

**Evidence must include novelty.** At least some evidence must come from contexts the learner has not previously encountered during instruction.

**No single form of evidence is sufficient.** Mastery is a multidimensional claim. Valid mastery claims require convergent evidence across multiple forms of observable demonstration.

---

## Progression and Mastery

Learning is a process; mastery is a state. Learners move through phases of partial understanding, productive confusion, overconfident performance, and gradual consolidation before stable mastery emerges.

Progression should be recognized and supported. A learner demonstrating mechanism understanding but not yet novel application is progressing — not failing. Assessment systems should provide useful diagnostic information at every stage, not only at the point where mastery is claimed.

---

## Grades and Mastery

Grades are institutional summaries. They compress performance across tasks, time, and criteria into a single symbol serving administrative functions.

Grades are not proof of mastery. The Open Mastery Standard does not prohibit grading. It establishes that grades and mastery claims are different kinds of statements. Where grading systems and mastery evidence coexist, the evidence record should be treated as the authoritative source of information about the learner's understanding.

---

## Relationship to the Open Mastery Framework

The Standard defines what mastery means. The Framework defines how learning is structured and navigated. They are distinct documents serving distinct functions. The Standard is philosophically and epistemically prior — it answers the question *what does it mean to genuinely understand something?* — and the Framework operates within the answer the Standard provides.
